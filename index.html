Thesis

The material world is the next frontier.

Capital has spent fifty years flooding software. AI now absorbs half of all global venture funding. The material world — energy, water, food, materials — remains largely unexplored and unmodernised at the molecular level.

Framework via Carlota Perez, Technological Revolutions and Financial Capital (2002)

Now

Reinventing the PhD to put venture at the heart of research.

I'm currently a candidate in the Venture Science Doctorate (VSD) at Deep Science Ventures — a fully-funded, 3-year programme that integrates scientific research with venture creation in climate, pharma, agriculture, and computation. I was selected as one of 5 candidates from over 600 applicants.

Unlike a traditional PhD, the VSD is designed from the start to produce a company. Candidates map a sector from first principles, develop and validate research hypotheses, and graduate as co-founders with IP transferred into a new venture — ready for investment from DSV's 400+ co-investor network.

My background in GTM, products, and B2B sales is rare in deep tech. My role is to do both jobs well: invent a genuine scientific breakthrough, then be the commercial engine that takes it to market at the speed it deserves.
